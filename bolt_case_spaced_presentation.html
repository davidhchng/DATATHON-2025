REVENUE ANALYSIS

REVENUE COMPOSITION ANALYSIS

Vancouver City FC follows a traditional sports club revenue model with stadium operations dominating at 67.2% of total revenue ($13.2M). The 32.8% merchandise revenue ($6.5M) represents significant growth opportunity.

Strategic Insight

The revenue composition suggests the club has successfully built a strong matchday experience, but diversification opportunities exist through merchandise optimization and digital enhancement.

MONTHLY REVENUE TRENDS

SEASONAL PATTERNS & STRATEGIC OPPORTUNITIES

The monthly revenue analysis reveals clear seasonal patterns that provide strategic opportunities for targeted marketing and operational planning.

  • February Peak: Stadium revenue reaches $3.96M, indicating high fan engagement during critical season moments
  • March Merchandise Surge: $1.09M in merchandise sales suggests strong post-season demand
  • Seasonal Planning: Clear cycles enable targeted marketing campaigns and inventory management

STADIUM OPERATIONS EFFICIENCY

OPERATIONAL EXCELLENCE ANALYSIS

Lower Bowl emerges as the most efficient revenue source, generating $2.1M in revenue. This success suggests a proven model for premium seating expansion and indicates strong demand for quality matchday experiences.

Strategic Opportunity

The Lower Bowl's success demonstrates that fans are willing to pay premium prices for enhanced experiences. This insight supports strategic investment in premium seating options and suggests opportunities for tiered pricing strategies across all stadium sections.

FAN ENGAGEMENT BY AGE GROUP

DEMOGRAPHIC ENGAGEMENT PATTERNS

Fan engagement shows remarkably consistent patterns across demographics with 5.7 games average attendance per member. This consistency suggests strong brand loyalty and community connection.

  • 26-40 Age Group: Highest engagement (5.8 games) - core demographic with purchasing power
  • 18-25 Age Group: Largest segment (44.8%) - key target for growth and long-term loyalty
  • Consistent Engagement: All age groups show similar attendance patterns, indicating universal appeal

SEASONAL PASS IMPACT ANALYSIS

THE GAME CHANGER - 5X ENGAGEMENT MULTIPLIER

This is the most significant finding of our entire analysis:

  • Seasonal Pass Holders: 22.4 games attended per season
  • Non-Holders: 4.5 games attended per season
  • 5x Engagement Multiplier: Seasonal pass holders are 5x more engaged
  • Current Adoption: Only 6.8% of members have seasonal passes
  • Expansion Potential: Massive opportunity for growth and revenue increase

MERCHANDISE PERFORMANCE BY CATEGORY

PRODUCT CATEGORY PERFORMANCE

Merchandise sales total $6.5M with strong growth potential, representing 32.8% of total revenue. The Jersey category dominates with $4.1M revenue, commanding premium pricing at $152 average.

Brand Value Assessment

The Jersey category's dominance and premium pricing ($152 average) demonstrates strong brand equity. Fans are willing to pay premium prices for authentic club merchandise, indicating deep emotional connection and brand loyalty.

SALES CHANNEL PERFORMANCE

DIGITAL TRANSFORMATION OPPORTUNITY

The online channel demonstrates 4x effectiveness compared to team store (80% vs 20% of sales), highlighting the critical importance of digital presence and e-commerce optimization.

  • Online Dominance: 80% of merchandise sales occur online
  • Team Store Underperformance: Only 20% of sales despite physical presence
  • Digital Opportunity: 4x advantage indicates massive potential for online expansion
  • E-commerce Optimization: Critical need for enhanced digital experience

PROMOTION STRATEGY ANALYSIS

PROMOTION STRATEGY CRISIS

Current promotions show 0.56x multiplier - actually reducing revenue effectiveness. This represents a major optimization opportunity that could significantly improve merchandise performance.

STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS

The underperformance suggests current promotion strategies are either poorly targeted, poorly timed, or offer insufficient value to drive meaningful sales increases. This represents one of the highest-impact optimization opportunities identified in our analysis.
